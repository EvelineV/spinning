Toe-up Stockinette Sock with Heel Flap Knitting Pattern

Instructions

Toe

Cast on {{ instructions.toe.castOnStitches }} stitches using Judy's magic cast on. Knit {{ instructions.toe.castOnStitches }} stitches for the first round. In the second round, (k2, M1L, knit to two stitches before end of needle, M1R, k2) * 2 for a total of {{ instructions.toe.castOnStitches + 4 }} stitches. Knit all stitches for every odd round. In every even round, increase four stitches (as in the second round) until you have {{ instructions.foot.stitchesInRound }} stitches. This takes {{ instructions.toe.rows }} rounds.

Foot

Keep knitting stockinette with rounds of {{ instructions.foot.stitchesInRound }} stitches for {{ instructions.foot.rows }} rounds.

Gusset

Divide all stitches over two needles, the instep stitches are on one needle, the heel stitches are on the second needle. The instep needle is always knitted as-is: K{{ instructions.foot.stitchesInRound/2 }}. On the heel needle, increase two stitches every odd round (k2, M1L, knit to two stitches before end, M1R, k2) until you reach {{ instructions.heel.gussetStitchesAtWidest }} stitches, which will take {{ instructions.heel.gussetRows }} rounds.

Heel

On the heel needle, you now have {{ instructions.heel.gussetStitchesAtWidest - instructions.foot.stitchesInRound/2}} stitches. Knit the first {{ instructions.heel.flapRows }} stitches, then place a stitch marker. Then knit the next {{ instructions.heel.startTurnStitches }} stitches and place a second stitch marker. There should be {{ instructions.heel.flapRows }} left on the left heel needle. You will now be working German Short Rows. Turn the work, create the double stitch and purl back. Knit and purl back and forth (create a double stitch on the first stitch of each row) until only the middle {{ instructions.heel.endTurnStitches }} stitches are left. You are now ready for a knit row.

Knit the four middle stitches, then knit each short row stitch and its wrap separately. Knit the last together with the first gusset stitch beyond the marker (use an SSK and remove the marker). Turn the work, purl until encountering a double stitch. Purl each stitch separate from its double, and use a p2tog to unite the last stitch and the first gusset stitch.

You should now have {{ instructions.foot.stitchesInRound / 2 }} stitches in the heel flap. On the knit rows, slip every odd stitch and knit every even stitch, use an SSK to incorporate a gusset stitch at the end of the row. On the purl rows, purl all the stitches, use a p2tog to incorporate a gusset stitch at the end of the row. When you have {{ instructions.foot.stitchesInRound / 2 + 1 }} stitches left on the heel needle, knit the instep needle. At the beginning of the next heel needle, knit the last gusset stitch together with the first stitch of the heel flap.

Leg

Knit the leg of the sock in stockinette with {{ instructions.roundLegStitches }} stitches in the round. When the sock is high enough, make a ribbed cuff and finish with an elastic bindoff.
